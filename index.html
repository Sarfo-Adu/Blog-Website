The hormone that makes you happy and depressed.

Written by: Isaac Sarfo-Adu


Have you ever felt extremely happy and wondered “what in in my body is causing these reactions?” On the flip side, have you also ever felt so sad and depressed and wondered if there is a substance in your body out there to hurt you? That hero and villain is Serotonin.


...

Serotonin is part of a class of hormones known as “the feel good hormones”. Hormones are chemical substances that are made in one part of the body, then move to other organs of the body and act as a signal to cause certain changes to the body, or act on the same cell or organ that produced it. The feel good hormones, as the name implies, are hormones that make you happy or gives pleasure while reducing sadness, depression or anxiety. They include, among others serotonin, dopamine, endorphins and oxytocin. Since they are a class of hormones, they mostly perform similar functions in making you happy, but serotonin in particular, has been shown to play a role in depression and subsequently in healing clinically depressed patients.


Serotonin, just like other hormones or neurotransmitters, are produced at the synaptic knob (the “tail”) of a neuron, and released into the synaptic cleft (the space between two neurons) inside a vesicle which acts as a transporter. It then binds to a receptor on the other neuron, and causes a change in the body. When serotonin levels are abundant in the synaptic cleft, studies show that it causes happiness and pleasure, just as the other feel good hormones do. On the other hand, when serotonin levels are reduced in the synaptic cleft, it may lead to clinical depression and sadness. Certain social occurrences may cause the serotonin production to reduce and hence cause depression. These occurrences could be stress, loss of a loved one, heartbreak, just to mention a few. One may ask, “If that is the case, then why not just inject the hormone into the blood to solve the depression?” Well that is not how it works, and serotonin can’t be manufactured and injected. Scientists came up with a different solution.


You see, in this synaptic cleft as mentioned earlier, there are stuff that carry the neurotransmitters and hormones, in this case serotonin, from the neuron with the receptor, back up into the neuron that produced it. This is known as “Reuptake”. In view of this, scientists introduced a drug that will be able to prevent or inhibit the reuptake of serotonin into the nerve cell that produced it, and in the process, ensuring that the amount of serotonin in the synaptic cleft is sufficient enough. This process will help treat depression.


Isn’t that fascinating, that such mechanisms exist in our body and these are some measures taken by scientists to solve problems as serious as depression, something people think is not treatable or nothing serious?
